The Hashino-era Trinity of the Persona Series

Katsura Hashino directed three games in the Persona series: Persona 3, Persona 4, and Persona 5. The three modern Persona games are the games which brought the series massive success. Hashino's Persona games are known for their unique blend of dungeon-crawling RPG gameplay and social simulation elements, as well as their exploration of mature and complex themes.

In this survey, participants have been questioned which title of the modern Persona games is their most preferred:

Persona 3

The game follows a high school student who discovers they have the ability to summon manifestations of their psyche, known as Personas, to fight creatures known as Shadows. The game has a dark tone and explores themes such as death, loss, and the meaning of life.

Persona 4

The game takes place in a rural Japanese town and follows a group of high school students as they investigate a series of murders that are connected to a mysterious TV world. Persona 4 explores themes such as identity, truth, and the importance of facing one's inner demons. It has a lighter tone than Persona 3 and features more lighthearted moments and humor.

Persona 5

The game follows a high school student who becomes involved in a vigilante group known as the Phantom Thieves of Hearts, who use their ability to summon Personas to "steal" the corrupted hearts of adults in order to reform society. Persona 5 explores themes such as rebellion, justice, and the consequences of one's actions. It has a stylish and slick aesthetic, with a focus on stealth gameplay.
